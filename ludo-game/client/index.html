Ludo Game Rules – Formal Explanation

Ludo is a strategy board game played by two to four players. Each player is assigned a distinct color and begins the game with four tokens placed in their respective starting area, known as the base.

The objective of the game is to move all four tokens from the base, around the board, and into the central home area before any other player.

The game begins with players taking turns rolling a single six-sided die. In order to move a token out of the base and onto the starting square, a player must roll a six or a one. Upon rolling a six or one, the player may either introduce a new token onto the board or move an existing token. Additionally, rolling a six grants the player an extra turn.

Once a token is on the board, it moves forward along a fixed path according to the number rolled on the die. The path follows a clockwise direction around the board.

If a player’s token lands on a square occupied by an opponent’s token, the opponent’s token is captured and returned to its base. However, certain squares designated as safe zones protect tokens from being captured.

After completing one full circuit of the board, a token enters its corresponding home column and proceeds toward the center. A token can only reach the final home position with an exact roll of the die.

The game continues with players alternating turns until one player successfully moves all four of their tokens into the home area. That player is declared the winner.
